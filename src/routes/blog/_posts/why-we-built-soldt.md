When we started our first online store in 2022, we used one of the big international e-commerce platforms. It worked — sort of. But every step felt like fighting against the system.

## The Problems We Kept Hitting

**Payments were a nightmare.** Setting up South African payment gateways meant third-party plugins, extra fees, and constant workarounds. Customers wanted to pay with SnapScan or EFT, and our platform had no idea what those were.

**Delivery was disconnected.** We had to manually copy tracking numbers between systems. There was no native integration with The Courier Guy, Pargo, or any local delivery service. Everything was built for USPS and FedEx.

**WhatsApp was invisible.** Our customers lived on WhatsApp. But our "e-commerce platform" had never heard of it. No order notifications, no abandoned cart recovery, no quick support — nothing.

## Building What We Needed

That's when we decided to build SOLDT. Not another Shopify clone — a platform that starts from the South African seller's perspective:

- **Local payments first** — Paystack, Yoco, SnapScan, Ozow, and EFT built in from day one
- **Delivery that just works** — integrated with the couriers SA sellers actually use
- **WhatsApp-native** — because that's where business happens in Africa

## What's Next

We're now in public beta, and the response has been incredible. Over 2,500 stores have launched on SOLDT, and we're just getting started.

If you're a South African seller frustrated with tools that weren't built for you — [give SOLDT a try](https://admin.soldt.co.za/signup). It's free to start.
