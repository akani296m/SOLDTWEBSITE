## 1. Shopify — Best Global All-In-One Ecommerce Platform

Shopify is one of the most widely used ecommerce platforms in the world. Millions of businesses use Shopify to build online stores, manage inventory, and process orders.

The platform provides a full toolkit for launching and scaling ecommerce businesses, with a massive ecosystem of apps, themes, and integrations.

However, Shopify is primarily designed for global markets, meaning South African businesses may need to rely on third-party apps to connect local payment gateways and shipping providers.

### Key Features

- Professional storefront themes
- Large app marketplace
- Advanced inventory management
- Global payment integrations
- Built-in analytics and reporting

### Pros

- Extremely scalable platform
- Huge ecosystem of developers and apps
- Supports international selling

### Cons

- Pricing starts around **$39/month (~R720+)**
- Many important features require paid apps
- Local South African integrations may require configuration

Shopify works best for brands planning to sell internationally or businesses that want access to a large ecosystem of tools and integrations.

## 2. Wix — Best for Simple Online Stores

Wix is primarily known as a website builder but also includes ecommerce functionality.

Its drag-and-drop editor makes it one of the easiest platforms to use for beginners who want to create visually appealing websites with built-in online store capabilities.

Wix works well for small product catalogs and businesses where design and branding are a priority.

However, as stores grow, some businesses find Wix's ecommerce tools less powerful compared to dedicated commerce platforms.

### Key Features

- Visual drag-and-drop website builder
- Customizable website templates
- Built-in ecommerce store functionality
- Product and inventory management

### Pros

- Very beginner friendly
- Strong design customization
- Quick setup process

### Cons

- Limited scalability for larger stores
- Less advanced ecommerce infrastructure
- Fewer integrations compared to major platforms

Wix is best suited for creators, freelancers, and small businesses launching their first online store.

## 3. Ecwid — Best for Adding Ecommerce to an Existing Website

Ecwid is designed for businesses that already have a website and want to add ecommerce functionality without rebuilding everything.

Instead of replacing your current website builder, Ecwid integrates into existing platforms like WordPress, Wix, and Squarespace.

This allows businesses to quickly add a shopping cart, product pages, and checkout functionality.

### Key Features

- Embeddable ecommerce widgets
- Multi-channel selling
- Global payment integrations
- Easy catalog management

### Pros

- Easy to integrate with existing websites
- Fast setup process
- Flexible deployment options

### Cons

- Limited advanced ecommerce capabilities
- Requires additional tools for scaling
- Not as powerful as full ecommerce platforms

Ecwid is best for businesses that want to **add ecommerce to an existing website rather than build a new store from scratch**.

## Important Features to Look For in a South African Ecommerce Platform

When choosing an ecommerce platform in South Africa, there are several key factors to consider.

### Payment Gateway Integrations

Online stores in South Africa typically rely on local payment providers such as PayFast, Ozow, Yoco, Peach Payments, and SnapScan.

Your ecommerce platform should integrate easily with these providers so customers can pay using EFT, cards, or local payment methods.

If you're evaluating payment providers, see our guide to [best payment gateways for South African online stores](/blog/best-payment-gateways-south-africa).

---

### Shipping and Logistics

Shipping is another critical component of ecommerce in South Africa.

Common logistics providers include The Courier Guy, Pargo, Aramex, and Fastway Couriers. Platforms that integrate directly with these providers can automate order fulfilment and tracking.

---

### Mobile Optimisation

South Africa is a **mobile-first ecommerce market**, meaning most customers browse and purchase products on their phones. Your ecommerce platform should automatically generate mobile-optimised storefronts.
