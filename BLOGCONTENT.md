# **How to Start an Online Store in South Africa (Step-by-Step Guide)**

Starting an online store in South Africa has never been more possible.

More South Africans are shopping online than ever before — from skincare and fashion to electronics and home goods. But while the opportunity is huge, many people still don’t know where to start.

Questions like:

* How do I accept payments online?

* Which courier should I use?

* Do I need a website developer?

* How much does it cost to start?

The good news is that launching an online store today can be done in **a matter of hours — not weeks**.

In this guide, we’ll walk through the **exact steps to start an online store in South Africa**, from choosing what to sell to accepting your first order.

---

# **Step 1: Choose What You Want to Sell**

The first step is deciding **what your online store will sell**.

Some of the most successful ecommerce businesses in South Africa sell:

* Skincare and beauty products

* Fashion and streetwear

* Fitness supplements

* Hair products and wigs

* Home decor and accessories

* Electronics accessories

A good product usually has three things:

1. **Clear demand** – people already want it

2. **Healthy margins** – enough profit after shipping and fees

3. **Strong branding potential** – something people can recognise

If you already sell products through **WhatsApp or Instagram**, you already have a great starting point.

Many South African businesses begin informally and then move to a proper online store once orders increase.

---

# **Step 2: Choose an Ecommerce Platform**

An ecommerce platform is the software that powers your online store.

It allows you to:

* Display products

* Accept payments

* Manage orders

* Track customers

* Handle shipping

Some popular platforms include:

* Shopify

* WooCommerce

* Wix

* SOLDT

For South African sellers, it’s important to choose a platform that supports:

* **Local payment gateways**

* **ZAR currency**

* **Local shipping options**

* **POPIA compliance**

Platforms built specifically for the South African market often make this much easier.

For example, **SOLDT allows merchants to launch an online store in minutes**, with built-in payment and shipping integrations designed for South Africa.

---

# **Step 3: Register Your Domain Name**

Your domain is your store’s web address.

Example:

yourbrand.co.za

A good domain should be:

* Short

* Easy to remember

* Brand focused

Many ecommerce platforms allow you to:

* Connect a domain from another provider

* Or purchase one directly inside your store dashboard.

For South African brands, **.co.za domains are usually the best choice**.

---

# **Step 4: Add Your Products**

Next, you’ll add the products you want to sell.

Each product listing should include:

### **Product title**

Clear and descriptive.

Example:

Hydrating Vitamin C Face Serum – 30ml

---

### **Product photos**

High-quality images dramatically increase sales.

Try to include:

* Product shot

* Close-up
